Tsum Valley, located in Nepal's Gorkha District, is a secluded region known for its rich Tibetan culture, stunning landscapes, and spiritual significance. Home to the indigenous Tsumbas, the valley is dotted with ancient monasteries like Mu Gompa and Rachen Gompa, and features chortens, mani walls, and prayer flags, preserving a way of life deeply rooted in Buddhism. Surrounded by the majestic peaks of the Ganesh Himal and Sringi Himal ranges, Tsum Valley offers breathtaking views and a serene environment. The trekking routes take you through lush forests, terraced fields, and high mountain passes, providing an off-the-beaten-path experience. Trekkers can interact with local communities, experiencing their traditional lifestyles and warm hospitality. Considered a sacred pilgrimage site, Tsum Valley is believed to be a Beyul, a hidden valley of happiness, blessed by Guru Rinpoche. It is a place of spiritual practice, with pilgrims visiting meditation caves associated with the Tibetan saint Milarepa.
